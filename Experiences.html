Experiences around Saas-Fee

A private base for every season in Saas-Fee.

From glacier skiing and winter sledging to summer hikes, marmots, mountain lakes, rope parks, and slow alpine days, Saas-Fee offers something for every kind of group. Fletschhorn gives you a private alpine base above the village, close to the experiences that make the valley special.

The area

A mountain region built for groups, families, skiers, hikers, and slow days.

Saas-Fee is surrounded by eighteen 4,000-metre peaks. Use the map, filters, activity cards, routes, and day plans to shape each day around your group.

Private base, active valley

The house is calm; the region is full.

The stay does not depend on one single activity. Guests can split by age, weather, energy level, or season, then return to one private house at the end of the day.

Safety note: mountain conditions change quickly. Route openings, lifts, weather, and guide requirements should always be checked with the official Saas-Fee/Saastal information before guests go.

Families, groups & retreats

Easy activities for mixed ages. Shared experiences for groups.

Families and multi-generational groups can choose from gentle walks, playground areas, cable-car outings, marmot encounters, winter snow play, treasure hunts, and village-based activities.

For families

Separate and reconnect easily.

Some guests can ski while others choose Aqua Allalin, village walks, a cable-car viewpoint, or a gentler forest trail. The advantage of Fletschhorn is that the group has one private place to come back to.

For retreats

Activity and reflection in the same valley.

For retreats, companies, and private groups, Saas-Fee offers a natural setting for both movement and calm: guided hikes, ski sessions, team challenges, wellness time, or slower moments in the village and forest.

Ready-made rhythms

Simple day plans guests can understand in seconds.

Use these sample rhythms to imagine a full day around the house, from slow family time to active mountain plans.

Soft family day

Village + marmots + pool

  1. Start slow at the house.
  2. Walk or take transport toward the village and lifts.
  3. Visit Spielboden for marmots when in season.
  4. End with Aqua Allalin or a calm village walk.

Active summer day

Hannig + themed trail + scooters

  1. Take the Hannig lift when operating.
  2. Use the Eddie trail or a scenic descent.
  3. Add mountaincarts or Trotti scooters for the group.
  4. Return to the house before evening.

Winter group day

Ski + sledging + warm reset

  1. Morning skiing, snowboarding, or ski school.
  2. Village break for mixed groups.
  3. Night sledging if available and safe.
  4. Return to the house as the private base.

Planning your stay

Build the rhythm that fits your group.
